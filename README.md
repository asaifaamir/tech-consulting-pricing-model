---

## Overview

A comprehensive pricing benchmarking and market analysis engagement 
for a technology consulting firm, covering **128 services across 6 
departments**. The objective was to replace intuition-based pricing 
with a structured, data-driven framework grounded in real market 
economics — giving the client a defensible, repeatable foundation 
for pricing decisions.

The central finding: the firm was materially underpriced relative 
to the market across virtually every service category, representing 
a significant and immediately actionable revenue opportunity.

---

## The Problem

The client had no structured pricing framework. Rates had drifted 
over time with no systematic reference to market benchmarks or 
delivery economics. This created three compounding issues:

- **Revenue leakage** — prices significantly below what the market 
  would bear
- **No floor protection** — some services priced below their direct 
  labor cost
- **No repeatable process** — pricing remained ad hoc with nothing 
  to update over time

---

## Methodology

### Three-Tier Valuation Hierarchy

Each of the 128 services was evaluated using one of three methods, 
applied in priority order based on available data:

**Tier 1 — Bottom-Up Workload Valuation (High Confidence)**  
Applied to Technical Consulting services where delivery hours by 
seniority were available.

Labor Cost = (Junior Hours × $75) + (Senior Hours × $125) + (Principal Hours × $175)


Billing rates reflect market-standard rates for U.S. technology 
consulting professionals, sourced from Clutch.co, GoodFirms, and 
Upwork Pro. Labor cost retained as cost floor — if Recommended 
Price falls below Labor Cost, a pricing floor violation is flagged.

**Tier 2 — Peer Anchor Benchmarking (Medium Confidence)**  
Applied to all other services. Market averages sourced from 
published tech agency rate cards, industry association benchmarks, 
and verified project pricing databases.

**Tier 3 — Composite SMB Default (Low Confidence)**  
Not required — **zero services** needed this method. Every service 
was successfully anchored to either hours data or peer benchmarks.

---

### Recommended Price Formula

Recommended Price = Market Average × 0.95


Applied uniformly across all 128 services. Positions the firm 5% 
below the market midpoint — competitive value proposition while 
capturing substantial market value.

| Sheet | Contents |
|---|---|

| **Service Pricing** | All 128 services with current price, labor cost (Tier 1), market avg, recommended price, and pricing position |
| **Pricing Opportunity** | Revenue impact analysis sorted by annual uplift — highest opportunity first |
| **Tier 1 — Hours Detail** | Dedicated sheet for 12 Technical Consulting services — full hours breakdown, labor cost, gross margin analysis |
| **Department Summary** | Aggregated view by department — pricing position distribution and total revenue opportunity |

## Key Findings

### Pricing Position Distribution
The majority of services were priced below market average, with 
the largest concentration in the Significantly Under category — 
representing the most immediate repricing opportunity.

### Tier 1 Highlight — Technical Consulting
The hours-based services demonstrated the most transparent margin 
analysis — showing exactly what each engagement costs to deliver 
versus what the market pays, with gross margin calculated for 
every service.

### Department Summary
The Department Summary sheet provides a birds-eye view of where 
the largest revenue gaps exist by practice area, enabling 
prioritized repricing decisions without reviewing every individual 
service.

## Strategic Recommendations

**Immediate**
- Reprice any services below direct labor cost — minimum 
  defensible price is labor cost
- Apply market-based recommended prices to all new client 
  engagements immediately
- Address any unpriced services — market averages computed, 
  zero client impact

**Short-Term**
- Develop phased repricing roadmap for existing clients with 
  advance notice
- Protect technology service rates — AI/ML and advanced 
  development services command premium rates

**Long-Term**
- Build annual pricing review into the business cycle — model 
  designed to be refreshed as market conditions evolve
- Consider premium positioning above market midpoint — strong 
  delivery track record supports it
